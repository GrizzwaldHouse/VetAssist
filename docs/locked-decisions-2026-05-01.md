## 1. VA accreditation: the dual path is realistic, with one structural change

**Bottom line: Pursue VSO sponsorship through DAV's CSO/volunteer track AND submit VA Form 21a for the claims agent exam this week.** The DAV NSO route requires a 16-month training program — not viable in 60–90 days — but the CSO/DSO volunteer track plus the claims agent exam can run in parallel, with the agent exam likely arriving 4–8 weeks after the OGC approves Marcus to sit.

### What the regulations actually require

VSO representative accreditation runs under **38 C.F.R. § 14.629(a)**: the sponsoring VSO files **VA Form 21** certifying Marcus is a member in good standing or a paid employee working ≥1,000 hours/year, and that he is *not* a federal employee (the federal-employee bar is absolute — confirm Marcus is fully separated, including from Reserve duty status). The claims agent path runs under **§ 14.629(b)** via **VA Form 21a** (OMB 2900-0605, expires 04/30/2028), submitted to OGC at 810 Vermont Avenue NW, Washington DC 20420, or by email to **ogcaccreditationmailbox@va.gov**.

OGC's review process recently shifted: **character references are now requested only after the applicant passes the claims agent exam**, which compresses the front-end timeline. Reported end-to-end time for VSO rep accreditation once a sponsor submits the package: **4–6 weeks**. Claims agent end-to-end (background check, exam, references, decision): **6–12 months**. There is no application or exam fee.

### Exam format (current, post-COVID)

The exam is **40 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes, closed-book, online proctored, no question revisits, 75% (30/40) to pass**, retakeable only twice in any 6-month period (§ 14.629(b)(6)). Recommended study material is the language of the regulations themselves: **38 U.S.C. Chapters 1, 11, 13, 15, 51, 53, 59, 71, 72** plus **38 C.F.R. Parts 1, 3, 4, 14, 19, 20**. VA does not publish or endorse a study guide; LongTermCareLink/NCPC reports a ~95% pass rate using ~40 hours of study, but for someone without a legal background **plan 50–70 hours**. At 30–45 minutes/day for 60–90 days Marcus gets 30–67 hours — adequate but tight; **bumping to 45 min/day minimum and a 75–90 day window is the safer plan**.

### Sponsorship realities at DAV and American Legion

**DAV** offers four representative tiers, but only the **Chapter Service Officer (CSO) or Department Service Officer (DSO) volunteer track** is feasible inside 90 days; eligibility requires DAV membership (Marcus qualifies as a wartime-era service-connected disabled veteran) plus chapter sponsorship. The NSO paid-employee path requires a 16-month structured training program. Contact: **DAV NSO Hotline 888-604-0234**, NSO locator at **locators.dav.org/NsoLocator**, or directly email the supervisor at the nearest DAV NSO office (e.g., the LA Regional Office DAV NSO if Marcus is in California).

**American Legion** restructured its training in 2024 into the **American Legion Accreditation & Training School** with Bergmann & Moore. Course sequence: **VA Disability Claims Processing 101** (virtual, 9×/year), **201** (March, annual), **202 Appeals** (March, annual). All require nomination from a Department Adjutant — Marcus cannot self-enroll. Process: Form 21 + Legion membership + DD-214 to local Post → Department Adjutant endorses → VSC Chair → Department Service Officer Training Committee → online module → recommendation forwarded to American Legion Veterans Affairs & Rehabilitation Division → VA OGC. Realistic end-to-end: **8–12 weeks** before in-classroom Basic Training.

### Study plan structured for the exam

The **highest-leverage study order** for someone with no legal background:

1. Weeks 1–2 (~7–10 hrs): **38 C.F.R. §§ 14.626–14.637** plus 38 U.S.C. Ch. 59 — ethics and representation. These cover ~7–8 of 40 exam questions and are heavily weighted.
2. Weeks 3–5 (~12–15 hrs): **38 C.F.R. Part 3** with focus on §§ 3.102 (benefit of doubt), 3.155 (claims), 3.159 (duty to assist), 3.303–3.310 (service connection direct/secondary/presumptive), 3.400 (effective dates — heavily tested), plus 38 U.S.C. Ch. 11/13/15.
3. Weeks 6–7 (~8–10 hrs): **Part 4** focusing on §§ 4.1, 4.7, 4.16 (TDIU), 4.25 (combined ratings — "VA math"), 4.26 (bilateral factor) plus one or two body-system schedules for structure (don't memorize every diagnostic code).
4. Weeks 8–9 (~6–8 hrs): Parts 19/20 (appeals, AMA modernization) plus 38 U.S.C. Ch. 51/53/71/72.
5. Weeks 10–12 (~8–10 hrs): Practice exams (LongTermCareLink offers 600 questions), wrong-answer review, M21-1 Part III for context.

### The legal firewall under § 14.636 and California SB 694

**38 C.F.R. § 14.636** is what keeps Marcus's accredited practice clean: VSO reps charge **zero fees ever**. Claims agents may charge **only after the AOJ issues an initial decision and a Notice of Disagreement is filed (legacy) or in modernized AMA review**. Fees are presumed reasonable at ≤20% of past-due benefits, presumed unreasonable above 33⅓%, and direct-pay fee agreements must be filed with OGC within 30 days of execution. **Marcus cannot charge any fee on an initial claim, ever, on either path.**

**California SB 694 was signed by Governor Newsom on February 10, 2026** (this changed during this research project; the bill chaptered after Marcus's initial planning) and amends Civil Code §1770 and Military & Veterans Code §401. Most provisions take effect, with the **predatory-contract voiding date set at January 1, 2027**. The statute prohibits charging any fee for VA claim preparation/presentation/prosecution except as federal law allows, prohibits fees exceeding what an accredited attorney/agent could charge, and **criminalizes requiring veterans to share login credentials** for VA.gov, eBenefits, DS Logon, milConnect, or any DoD/VA/DHS system. There is no explicit "educational tool safe harbor" — the safe harbor is implicit in the bill's scope, which targets *claim preparation*, not generic education.

The recommended firewall: **Marcus's accreditation lives under his personal name** (POAs filed via VA Form 21-22 / 21-22a); **VetAssist is a separate legal entity** that explicitly does not prepare, present, or prosecute claims, does not collect VA.gov credentials, charges only for software/SaaS access (never tied to claim outcomes), and conspicuously discloses non-accreditation in-product. Marcus must not use his VA accreditation to market the platform — VA OGC has explicitly forbidden this.

## 2. Hugging Face pipeline: three substitutions before code freeze

**Bottom line: Replace toxic-bert with a fine-tuned MentalRoBERTa, replace WhisperSpeech with Kokoro-82M, and treat Bio_ClinicalBERT as a base model that needs a fine-tuned head — not a turnkey NER service.** Otherwise the architecture is sound and the cost story is gentle: a mixed strategy (Groq for Whisper, self-hosted Kokoro and BERTs on a $80/mo CPU box, pgvector for embeddings) lands at roughly **$130/mo at pre-revenue scale**.

### Speech-to-text: Whisper turbo via Groq

**openai/whisper-large-v3-turbo** has 809M parameters (decoder pruned from 32 → 4 layers vs. v3's 1.54B), runs ~6–8× faster than v3 with only ~2 percentage points of WER regression on English, and on Groq hits 216× real-time at **$0.04/hour of audio** — dramatically cheaper than the OpenAI Whisper API at $0.36/hour. Self-hosting via faster-whisper INT8 on an RTX 3060 12GB (or even modern x86 CPU) is feasible as fallback. **Recommendation: Groq for production, self-hosted faster-whisper-large-v3-turbo on a single L4 endpoint as HIPAA-sensitive backup.** Plan a small LoRA fine-tune on veteran audio later — military jargon (OEF/OIF, MOS, VBA) will degrade the generic WER otherwise.

### PII: Presidio with custom recognizers

Presidio-analyzer **2.2.362** supports Python 3.10–3.13, ships built-in recognizers for SSN, date, email, phone, US driver's license, and includes a `MedicalNERRecognizer` based on `blaze999/Medical-NER` for PHI. **Custom recognizers are fully supported via PatternRecognizer** — Marcus needs to build YAML configs for VA File Number (8–9 digits, often "C-" prefix, context words "VA file"/"claim"/"C-file"), Service Number (pre-1969 patterns like "RA12345678"), EDIPI (10-digit DoD ID), and DD-214 markers. Run as a FastAPI service in `apps/ml-pipeline/pii-service`. Note the 2025 breaking change: **Presidio's hash anonymizer now defaults to random salt** — set explicit salt if Marcus needs referential integrity across sessions.

### Crisis detection: ditch toxic-bert immediately

This is the most important model decision in the pipeline. **toxic-bert was trained on Jigsaw Wikipedia comments** to detect insult/profanity/threat directed at others — it produces near-zero scores on the actual language patterns of veteran crisis ("I don't see a way out," "I'd be better off…," "after what happened in Iraq…"), which are calm and declarative. Using it as a crisis gate creates dangerous false negatives.

The right architecture is a tiered detector:

- **Tier 1: Fine-tuned MentalRoBERTa.** `mental/mental-roberta-base` (or `AIMH/mental-roberta-large`) was pretrained on r/SuicideWatch, r/depression, r/Anxiety, r/offmychest, and r/bipolar — ~13.6M sentences — and outperforms BERT, BioBERT, and ClinicalBERT on suicide-ideation/depression/stress detection. Fine-tunes hit 95–99% accuracy on r/SuicideWatch tasks. Use a 4-level ordinal head (indicator/ideation/behavior/attempt, per arXiv 2510.20085).
- **Tier 2: Claude with a SAMHSA-aligned system prompt** for nuanced escalation, with a 988-aware response template.
- **Tier 3: Hard rule-based regex** for explicit attempt markers (method + timeline) → immediate human handoff.

**No officially VA-partnered open crisis model exists on HF Hub as of April 2026** — REACH VET is internal. Closest research artifacts are **TIDE** (PTSD synthetic, arXiv 2505.15065), **Thousand Voices of Trauma** (3,000 PE-therapy synthetic, arXiv 2504.13955), and **MentalChat16K** on HF. ⚠️ The `Amod/mental_health_counseling_conversations` dataset has a RAIL-D license requiring donation for commercial use. Toxic-bert can stay as a secondary filter for abuse directed at staff — never as the primary gate.

### Text-to-speech: Kokoro-82M, not WhisperSpeech

**WhisperSpeech has been overtaken in 2025–2026 by Kokoro-82M** (Apache-2.0, 14 built-in voices, sub-300ms latency, 36–96× real-time, runs on CPU) and **Chatterbox** (MIT, beats ElevenLabs in 63.75% of blind tests). **Avoid XTTS-v2** — its Coqui Public Model License is non-commercial. Recommended: **Kokoro-82M as primary** (deployable on a $40/mo Hetzner CPU box), with Chatterbox or F5-TTS on a self-hosted L4 endpoint when Marcus wants a branded voice clone using a licensed voice actor.

### Symptom extraction: Bio_ClinicalBERT is a base model, not an NER service

**`emilyalsentzer/Bio_ClinicalBERT` is a masked-LM only** — it does not produce NER labels without a fine-tuned classification head. It's also limited to a 512-token context, which is too short for many veteran testimonials. Better paths: **`blaze999/Medical-NER`** (ready-made, used inside Presidio's MedicalNERRecognizer), **`yikuan8/Clinical-Longformer`** for 4096-token veteran narratives, and **`UFNLP/gatortron-base/-medium/-large`** (trained on 90B-word UF Health corpus, outperforms Bio_ClinicalBERT by 7.5–9.6% on clinical NLI/QA). **Stage 1 (immediate): blaze999/Medical-NER + Clinical-Longformer.** Stage 2 (12+ months): GatorTron fine-tune on veteran-symptom labeled data with outputs mapped to SNOMED-CT/UMLS CUIs to bridge to 38 CFR.

### Semantic search to 38 CFR: BGE + reranker, not MiniLM

The MTEB 2025/2026 leaderboard puts **`BAAI/bge-large-en-v1.5`** (335M, MIT, 768-dim) and **`BAAI/bge-m3`** (568M, native dense+sparse fusion) at the sweet spot of price/performance for legal/regulatory text. CFR sections are dense, lexically specific, and clausal — pure-dense retrieval misses statutory matches; **hybrid retrieval (BM25 + dense, fused with reciprocal rank fusion) plus a cross-encoder reranker** (`BAAI/bge-reranker-v2-m3`) on top-50 candidates → top-5 is the right architecture. Skip `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` for production (only for prototype/edge). For long CFR sections, `nomic-embed-text-v1.5` (8K context, 137M, Apache-2.0) is the long-context option. *(Note: Area 6's research recommends voyage-law-2 specifically for legal embeddings — voyage-law-2 reports +10% NDCG@10 over OpenAI v3-large on three legal datasets and is worth A/B testing against bge-large-en-v1.5 for the CFR-specific corpus.)*

### Cost reality: $130/mo at pre-revenue scale

For 1,000 veterans/month at ~10 min audio + ~3KB testimony each, the recommended stack is **Claude API + Groq Whisper (~$7/mo) + self-hosted Kokoro on a $40/mo Hetzner CPU box + Presidio/BERTs/embeddings on a single $80/mo CPU server with FastAPI/uvicorn + pgvector inside the existing Postgres = ~$130/mo**. HF Inference Endpoints with scale-to-zero comes in at $150–$300/mo for the same workload. AWS self-host on a g5.xlarge A10G 24GB is $250–$500/mo all-in but consolidates infra.

Hugging Face does **not** run a formal startup grant program; the de facto "startup tier" is **PRO at $9/mo** (20× inference credits, 8× ZeroGPU quota, H200 ZeroGPU Spaces). **Community GPU Grants** are awarded inside Spaces settings ("sleep time settings" → "Apply for community GPU grant") for innovative public Spaces with academic/community-good utility — temporary and revocable if usage falls. Marcus should publish a public veteran-resource Space first, then apply.

### Turborepo architecture for Node + Python

**Python lives at `apps/ml-pipeline/`** with subdirectories per service (stt, pii, crisis, nlp, tts, orchestrator). Turborepo doesn't manage Python deps natively but does orchestrate tasks — use `poetry` per service with a `package.json` shim that runs `poetry run uvicorn …`. Type safety bridge: **FastAPI auto-generates OpenAPI; `@hey-api/openapi-ts` emits a typed TS client into `packages/sdk`** consumed by both Next.js and React Native — single source of truth. Service communication: **REST/HTTP between Node and Python**, with **Celery + Redis queue** for long-running pipelines (>10s) and SSE/WebSocket for streaming progress. Skip gRPC for MVP. Reference template: `cording12/next-fast-turbo`.

### The 10-stage pipeline, executable

```
Client (Next.js / RN) → apps/api (auth, audit, enqueue)
  → orchestrator FastAPI + Celery
    [1] STT — Groq whisper-large-v3-turbo
    [2] PII Scrub — Presidio + custom VA File/Service/EDIPI/DD-214 recognizers
    [3] Crisis Gate — fine-tuned MentalRoBERTa (4-level ordinal); HALT + 988 if ≥3
    [4] Event Extraction — Claude (structured JSON: incidents, dates, locations)
    [5] Symptom Mapping — Medical-NER / Clinical-Longformer → SNOMED/ICD-10
    [6] CFR Matching — bge-large-en-v1.5 + bge-reranker-v2-m3 over 38 CFR index
    [7] Narrative Structuring — Claude (cite-then-answer pattern)
    [8] TTS — Kokoro-82M (CPU)
    [9] Compliance Gate — rule engine + Claude review (PII residue, citation
        validity, SAMHSA crisis language)
    [10] Structured Output — versioned JSON + PDF + audio in encrypted S3
```

## 5. Compliance: AB 489 and SB 243 are MVP-blocking, FIPS 140-3 is not

**Bottom line: Five of the seven gates are MVP-blocking; FIPS 140-3 is a marketable trust signal but not legally required for a consumer-facing app; HIPAA likely does not apply but California CMIA and Washington MHMDA do.** The most consequential clarification: the bill Marcus called "AB 489 (2022)" is actually **AB 489 (2025), Chapter 615 — effective January 1, 2026** — and it is dramatically broader than AB 3030 because it applies to *any* AI deployer in California, not just covered healthcare entities.

### AB 489 (2025) — the AI-impersonation-of-clinicians bill

Codified at California Business & Professions Code §4999.8 et seq., effective January 1, 2026. The prohibition: any AI/GenAI system in advertising or functionality that "indicates or implies that the care, advice, reports, or assessments being offered through the AI or GenAI technology is being provided by a natural person in possession of the appropriate license or certificate to practice as a health care professional" — **with each use a separate violation** enforceable by state health-profession licensing boards. Applies to *any* AI deployer in California, not just clinics.

**Required actions:** Audit all UX copy, marketing, avatar names, system prompts, button labels, and error messages for clinical-titles language (Doctor, Physician, M.D., D.O., Psychologist, Therapist, Counselor, Nurse, RN, white-coat imagery, stethoscope, caduceus). The system prompt must prevent Claude from saying "As your doctor…" or "I'm a licensed therapist…" The persona name "VetAssist" is fine; "Dr. VetAssist" is not.

**Recommended verbatim disclaimer:** *"VetAssist is an AI-powered educational tool. It is not a doctor, licensed health care professional, attorney, or VA-accredited representative. The information provided is for general educational purposes only and is not medical, legal, or benefits-claim advice. Always consult a licensed professional or a VA-accredited representative for personalized advice."*

**AB 3030 likely does NOT apply** — it's restricted to "health facility, clinic, physician's office, or office of a group practice." Document this conclusion in the compliance file with legal review if VetAssist later partners with a covered entity (VAMC, FQHC, VSO clinic).

### SB 243 (2025) — companion chatbots, January 1, 2026

Codified at Business & Professions Code §22601 et seq.; reporting begins July 1, 2027. The "companion chatbot" definition: an AI system with a natural-language interface providing adaptive, human-like responses, capable of meeting a user's social needs, exhibiting anthropomorphic features, and sustaining a relationship across multiple interactions. Exclusions for customer service / business operations / productivity / standalone voice assistants apply only if those are the *only* uses.

**Conservative recommendation: assume VetAssist is in scope** if it has a name/personality, gives empathetic responses about veteran experiences (PTSD, MST, transition), sustains conversations across sessions, or markets itself as a "companion"/"buddy." Skadden, Perkins Coie, and FPF all advise this conservative read.

**Required disclosure (recommended verbatim):** *"You are chatting with VetAssist, an AI assistant. VetAssist is not a human, not a licensed professional, and not a VA-accredited representative."*

**Required crisis prevention protocol — published on vetassist.com:** Maintain a documented protocol for "preventing the production of suicidal ideation, suicide, or self-harm content"; provide referrals to crisis services (988, Crisis Text Line, **Veterans Crisis Line: 988 then Press 1; text 838255; chat veteranscrisisline.net/chat**); reference the protocol publicly.

**Penalties:** Civil action; damages = greater of actual damages or **$1,000 per violation**; injunctive relief; attorneys' fees. **Recommendation: default age-gate at 18+** to avoid the more onerous minor-user requirements (every-three-hours notifications, sexually-explicit-material safeguards) unless youth dependents are core to the product.

### WCAG 2.1 AA for AI chat — the failures that get apps rejected

The most common failures in AI chat UIs: streaming AI responses not announced to screen readers (no live region), focus stolen by new messages, typing indicators visible but not announced, color-only user/AI distinction, send button mouse-only, voice input button unlabeled. **Use `aria-live="polite"` (NOT assertive) on a `role="log"` transcript container; throttle/coalesce streaming token updates rather than announcing every token; use `aria-live="assertive"` + `role="alert"` only for errors and crisis intervention banners.** On widget open, focus moves to message input; on close, focus returns to launcher; new AI messages do NOT steal focus. All controls reachable via Tab; Enter sends, Shift+Enter newline; tap targets ≥44×44 px. Voice input requires `aria-pressed` state and a text-input alternative (WCAG 2.1.1). Captions/transcripts for any AI voice output (WCAG 1.2.1). Veterans have ~25% disability prevalence — accessibility is both legal and ethical core requirement. Run NVDA + JAWS + VoiceOver + TalkBack manual tests; automated tools catch only ~30% of issues.

### FIPS 140-3 — confirmed September 21, 2026 deadline, but not legally required for VetAssist

NIST CMVP moves all FIPS 140-2 certificates to "Historical" on September 21, 2026. **VetAssist as a consumer-facing app under no federal contract has no statutory FIPS obligation.** It becomes essential only if/when VetAssist pursues VA partnership, FedRAMP, government grants, or markets as "government-grade" security. **For MVP, do not spend engineering cycles pursuing FIPS validation.** Use TLS 1.2+ with strong ciphers (AES-256-GCM, ChaCha20-Poly1305); document use of "FIPS-approved algorithms"; plan post-Series A move to FIPS-validated modules. **Marketing caveat:** do NOT say "FIPS 140-3 compliant" without an actual CMVP certificate — that is a misleading claim. ## 6. Knowledge scraper: the technical pieces are solved; UPL is the existential risk

**Bottom line: eCFR API, Federal Register API, and VA Lighthouse APIs cover most ingestion needs without any scraping; CFR text is public domain under 17 U.S.C. § 105 and republication is settled law; the dominant risk is 38 U.S.C. § 5905 criminal exposure plus FTC AI-claim enforcement (DoNotPay precedent).** Architecture must enforce, not merely disclaim, the line between *information* and *claim-specific advice*.

### eCFR API — confirmed and sufficient

Documentation at **ecfr.gov/developers/documentation/api/v1**; base URL `https://www.ecfr.gov`; **no authentication required**; XML and JSON outputs; updated daily, "generally current within two business days." No formal published rate limit but 504 timeouts observed on large pulls — throttle to ~1 req/sec, retry with exponential backoff, prefer Part-level over Title-level fetches. Key calls for VetAssist:

- `GET /api/versioner/v1/full/{date}/title-38.xml?part=3` (compensation/pension/DIC)
- `GET /api/versioner/v1/full/{date}/title-38.xml?part=4` (rating schedule)
- `GET /api/versioner/v1/full/{date}/title-38.xml?part=14` (representation)
- `GET /api/versioner/v1/versions/title-38.json?issue_date[gte]={last_sync}` (diff detection)
- `GET /api/versioner/v1/structure/{date}/title-38.json` (hierarchy)
- `GET /api/versioner/v1/ancestry/{date}/title-38.json?section=3.304` (breadcrumbs)
- `GET /api/admin/v1/corrections.json?title=38` (editorial corrections audit)

Update detection pattern: daily cron polls `/titles.json`, checks `up_to_date_as_of` for Title 38; if advanced, query `/versions/title-38.json` filtered to last sync date; response lists every changed `identifier` (e.g., `3.350`); re-ingest only those sections. Subscribe to email alerts at ecfr.gov/my/subscriptions as redundant safety net. Reference impl: `github.com/sam-berry/ecfr-analyzer` (Go); Markdown corpus seed: `github.com/AlextheYounga/ecfr` (entire eCFR back to 2002, Git-versioned). GPO bulk XML mirror at gpo.gov/fdsys/bulkdata/ECFR is a fallback during NARA shutdowns.

### VA.gov scraping — robots.txt is permissive but use Lighthouse APIs first

VA.gov's robots.txt blocks only `/cgi-bin/`, `/drupal`, `/covid19screen`, and `/analytics-opt-out.html`; a polite identifying crawler with rate-limiting is technically compliant. **But developer.va.gov's Lighthouse APIs are the better path:**

- **VA Forms API** (`developer.va.gov/explore/api/va-forms/docs`) — current revision dates for 21-526EZ, 21P-534EZ (DIC), 26-1880 (Certificate of Eligibility), GI Bill forms, etc.
- **Facilities API** — VA medical facility addresses/services/hours for routing veterans to local resources.

Auth model: API key in `apikey` HTTP header, sandbox first, schedule a demo with VA, then production key. **Apply for sandbox access early to avoid launch delay.**

**M21-1 Manual** lives at the KnowVA portal (`knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/system/templates/selfservice/va_ssnew/help/customer/locale/en-US/portal/554400000001018/topic/554400000004049`). Page is JavaScript-rendered; needs Playwright/Puppeteer (brittle). VBA reorganized M21-1 in FY21; portal IDs have remained stable for years but plan for breakage. Subscribe to VA email updates as redundant signal. **M21-1 is internal VBA guidance subordinate to statute and 38 CFR — surface this to users; when CFR and M21-1 conflict, CFR wins.**

### Federal Register API

Docs at **federalregister.gov/developers/documentation/api/v1**; no auth required; JSON and CSV; pagination capped at first 2000 results per query (use date-range filters). Key call for VetAssist:

### Legal status of scraping and republishing

**17 U.S.C. § 105:** "Copyright protection under this title is not available for any work of the United States Government." Effect (per House Report): "place all works of the United States Government, published or unpublished, in the public domain." Limits to flag: contractor-authored content is NOT automatically public domain (some VA training videos, AMA tables referenced in 38 CFR Part 4, ICD-10 lookups can fall outside § 105 — quote sparingly with attribution); USPS is excluded from § 105 (irrelevant); foreign jurisdictions don't follow § 105 (irrelevant if US-only). The "edicts of government" doctrine (Banks v. Manchester; **Georgia v. Public.Resource.Org**, 590 U.S. 255 (2020)) reinforces that even when government uses third-party labor, the actual law/regulation cannot be copyrighted — strengthens safe harbor for republishing CFR.

**Feist v. Rural Tel.** (1991) — no "sweat-of-the-brow" protection; CFR is verbatim reproduction of laws → no compilation copyright.

**Scraping caselaw:** *hiQ v. LinkedIn* (9th Cir. 2019, reaffirmed April 2022) — scraping publicly accessible data is not a CFAA violation. *Van Buren v. United States* (2021) — narrows CFAA to access of system parts user not entitled to. *Meta v. Bright Data* (N.D. Cal. Jan. 2024) — scraping logged-out public data did not breach Meta's ToS. **Caveat: TOS contracts are still enforceable** (hiQ lost the breach-of-contract claim and paid $500K). Don't create fake accounts; don't bypass logged-in walls; read VA.gov / developer.va.gov ToS carefully.

### RAG architecture: hierarchical chunking, hybrid retrieval, two-stage rerank

**Chunking — preserve the section hierarchy.** For 38 CFR's eCFR XML (DIV1=Title, DIV5=Part, DIV6=Subpart, DIV8=Section, `<P>` paragraphs), the atomic unit is the **paragraph within a section** (e.g., 38 CFR § 3.304(f)(3)). **Prepend breadcrumb to embedded text** ("Title 38 > Part 3 > Subpart A > § 3.304 (Direct service connection; PTSD) > (f)(3)"); the legal-RAG literature shows this is among the highest-ROI changes. Hierarchical multi-layer indexing at Section/Subsection/Paragraph/Sub-paragraph levels lets "what's the rating criteria for tinnitus" hit § 4.87 DC 6260 directly while "how does PTSD service connection work" hits the whole § 3.304(f). 200–1000 token chunks; recursively split paragraphs that exceed. **Always store metadata:** `{title:38, part:3, subpart:"A", section:"3.304", paragraph:"(f)(3)", effective_date, cfr_url, source_xml_hash}`.

**Embeddings.** Recommend **voyage-law-2** as primary (1T legal token training; +10% NDCG@10 over OpenAI v3-large on three legal datasets; 16K context; ~$0.22/M tokens; Anthropic's preferred provider). Alternative: voyage-3-large (Matryoshka, +5.6% over OpenAI v3-large), or BGE-large-en-v1.5 for self-hosting. **Avoid legal-bert** (older, 512-token context, no longer SOTA). *(Note: this is the cleanest example in the report of cross-area input — Area 2 recommended bge-large-en-v1.5; Area 6's deeper legal-RAG research recommends voyage-law-2; A/B test both on the 200-pair golden set.)*

**Vector DB.** **pgvector in PostgreSQL** is the recommended option — single source of truth, transactional updates when CFR changes, native hybrid via `tsvector` + pgvector, HNSW indexes. Qdrant is the strong self-hosted alternative.

**Hybrid search.** BM25 + dense, fused with **Reciprocal Rank Fusion (k=60)** is the empirically best first stage — BM25 is essential because users type exact citation strings ("38 CFR 3.156", "DC 6260") that pure semantic search degrades on. **Reranking** with **Cohere Rerank v3.5/v4.0** or **Zerank-2** or self-hosted **bge-reranker-v2-m3** — two-stage hybrid → cross-encoder gives Recall@5 = 0.816 vs. 0.695 hybrid alone (+17.4%) per arXiv 2604.01733.

**Citation grounding (critical for legal accuracy).** Each chunk's metadata includes canonical citation and stable eCFR deep link (`https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-38/chapter-I/part-3/subpart-A/section-3.304#p-3.304(f)(3)`). **Cite-then-answer prompt:** *"Answer ONLY using the regulations provided below. After every factual claim, output a citation in the form `[§ 3.304(f)(3)]`. If the regulations do not address the question, say so. Do NOT invent rules. Do NOT translate criminal-penalty or fee provisions in §§ 5901–5905 — quote them verbatim."* Render every citation as a hyperlink. Display effective date alongside answer. Post-hoc regex + URL existence check before display — **reject answers with unverifiable cites; show "not found in source" rather than fabricate**.

**Update detection → vector invalidation.** Daily cron polls eCFR `/versions/title-38.json`. For each changed identifier: mark all vectors with that section as stale, fetch new XML, re-chunk, re-embed, atomically swap. Append-only audit log with `previous_hash`, `new_hash`, `FR_citation`, `human_reviewer_id`. Dashboard surfaces "X sections updated this week → Y plain-English summaries flagged for review."

**Human review gate (near-mandatory for compliance).** No plain-English summary auto-publishes. When a regulation changes: LLM regenerates summary, diff vs. previous summary highlighted, queue routes to a VA-accredited reviewer (Marcus once accredited) or subject-matter editor + counsel review, sign-off recorded. **This is the control that distinguishes VetAssist from DoNotPay.**

### UPL — the highest-risk area

The DoNotPay enforcement action defines the legal landscape. **FTC settlement September 25, 2024, finalized January 16, 2025:** DoNotPay marketed as "the world's first robot lawyer" generating "ironclad" demand letters; FTC complaint alleged the company never tested outputs against human-lawyer baseline and never hired/retained attorneys to review. Settlement: $193K monetary relief, prohibited from claiming AI service substitutes for human professionals without competent and reliable evidence, required notice to all 2021–2023 subscribers. California State Bar parallel cease-and-desist June 15, 2023. Future violations are expensive.

**Non-negotiable VetAssist takeaways:** never market as "AI lawyer," "AI advocate," "robot VSO," or anything that implies professional substitution; never use "ironclad," "guaranteed," "valid," or comparative-quality language without empirical backing; prominently disclose limits to every user upfront and in every relevant flow; document a testing methodology and a panel of accredited VSOs/attorneys reviewing outputs.

**LegalZoom's surviving disclaimer template** (pattern adopted near-universally in legal tech): *"LegalZoom is not a law firm and may not perform services performed by an attorney. LegalZoom's legal document service is not a substitute for the advice of an attorney. LegalZoom cannot provide legal advice and can only provide self-help services at your specific direction."* Pattern: not a law firm + not legal advice + self-help at your direction + consult a lawyer for your situation.

**VA-specific UPL is stricter than ordinary UPL.** **38 U.S.C. § 5901**: no person may act as agent/attorney in claim preparation/presentation/prosecution unless VA-accredited. **§ 5904**: attorneys/agents may charge fees only after a Notice of Disagreement is filed. **§ 5905**: criminal penalty (fine + ≤1 year prison) for charging fees in violation of § 5904 — including for *advice on how to file*. **38 CFR § 14.629(c)**: even staff of an accredited attorney can only assist under that attorney's supervision. **§ 14.630**: one-time non-accredited assistance exception covers a single veteran gratis — does not cover SaaS scaling to thousands.

VA OGC's published guidance line: "Anyone can talk about veterans benefits in general...need not be accredited." But: "The point at which discussion narrows down to specific advice concerning the veteran's service record, medical conditions, financial situation including income and assets and other issues directly relating to a claim specific to a veteran or dependent requires accreditation."

**The practical line VetAssist must draw:**

| ✅ Allowed without accreditation | ❌ Requires accreditation |
|---|---|
| "Here is what 38 CFR § 3.304(f) says about PTSD service connection." | "Based on your stressor and MOS, you qualify for service connection for PTSD." |
| "Here are the rating criteria in 38 CFR § 4.130 for mental disorders." | "Your symptoms suggest you should be rated at 70%." |
| "DIC eligibility requires the veteran's death to be service-connected (38 CFR § 3.5)." | "Your husband's COD will qualify for DIC; here's how to fill out 21P-534EZ." |
| Linking to VA forms via Forms API | Filling out forms with the user's data |
| "Education benefits transfer rules: see 38 CFR § 21.9570." | "Here's the strategy to maximize your transfer to your dependent." |

**Architectural enforcement** — the LLM system prompt must contain a hard refusal clause: *"Do not analyze the user's specific facts to advise on whether they qualify, what rating to claim, or how to fill out a form. If the user provides specific facts, redirect them to a VA-accredited representative or an accredited attorney via va.gov/ogc/apps/accreditation/. Quote the regulation only."* Personalization features (rating estimators, eligibility quizzes) need legal review before launch — they most likely require VA accreditation of the human in the loop or partnership with an accredited VSO. **Once Marcus has his own accreditation, the picture shifts: he can personally review specific-fact analyses produced by the LLM, signing off as the accredited human in the loop.** This is a strong reason to push the accreditation timeline aggressively.
