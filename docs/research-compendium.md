## 1. Federal Law: Veteran Claims Assistance Fee Prohibition

### Primary Authority

**38 U.S.C. § 5904 — Recognition of Agents and Attorneys Generally**
- Source: https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title38-section5904
- Key provision: Subsection (c)(1) prohibits fees for services provided before
  VA issues its initial decision on a claim.
- Subsection (a)(5): Fees must not exceed 20% of past-due benefits when charged
  under a direct-pay agreement.
- Subsection (d): VA may pay fees directly to agents/attorneys from past-due benefits.

**38 CFR § 14.636 — Payment of Fees for Representation**
- Source: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-38/chapter-I/part-14/subject-group-ECFRe2d861683c66a39/section-14.636
- Updated through: March 18, 2026 (last amendment February 27, 2026)
- Key provision: Subsection (c) — Agents and attorneys may charge fees ONLY after:
  (1) Agency of original jurisdiction has issued a decision
  (2) A Notice of Disagreement has been filed
  (3) Power of attorney and fee agreement are filed with VA
- Subsection (e): Fees must be "reasonable" at all times
- Subsection (f): Fees exceeding 33.33% of past-due benefits are presumed unreasonable

**38 CFR § 14.632(c)(5)-(6) — Standards of Conduct**
- Source: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-38/chapter-I/part-14/subject-group-ECFRe2d861683c66a39/section-14.632
- Prohibits VA-accredited individuals from entering into agreements to charge
  unreasonable fees or fees prohibited by law.
- Violation is grounds for cancellation of VA accreditation.

**VA Office of General Counsel — Tips on Fee Agreements**
- Source: https://www.va.gov/OGC/docs/Accred/TipsonFeeAgreementsforVeteransClaims.pdf
- Key finding: "Charging a fee or accepting a gift on an initial claim — including
  charging for assistance with gathering necessary documents and filling out forms —
  is a violation of the VA Standards of Conduct."

**38 U.S.C. § 5905 — Penalty for Certain Acts**
- Source: https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title38-section5905
- Historical note: Criminal penalties for unauthorized fee charging were removed by
  Public Law 109-461 (2006). This removal created the regulatory gap that enabled
  the "claim shark" industry.
- The GUARD VA Benefits Act (H.R. introduced 2022, 2023) proposed reinstating
  criminal penalties but has not passed as of April 2026.
- The CHOICE for Veterans Act advanced from House Committee on Veterans' Affairs
  (May 2025) — would establish certified consultant framework.

**National Organization of Veterans' Advocates (NOVA) — Fee Information**
- Source: https://www.vetadvocates.org/cpages/fees-which-may-be-charged-by-agent-attorney
- Summary: "Under current law, fees may not be charged by an agent or an attorney
  for work performed in connection with the filing of a claim for VA benefits."

### Relevance to VetAssist
VetAssist does NOT charge fees and does NOT represent veterans before the VA.
VetAssist is an educational platform providing informational tools. This positions
VetAssist outside the scope of 38 U.S.C. § 5904 fee restrictions entirely, but
the system must never drift into "preparation, presentation, or prosecution" of claims.

## 2. State Law: Veteran Protection Legislation (2024-2026)

### California — SB 694 (Signed February 10, 2026)

**Source:** https://www.gov.ca.gov/2026/02/10/governor-newsom-signs-consumer-protection-bill-cracking-down-on-veteran-fraud-and-abuse-launches-tax-cut-for-vets/

**Key provisions:**
- Prohibits unaccredited persons from directly or indirectly soliciting, contracting
  for, charging, or receiving any fee for preparation, presentation, or prosecution
  of VA claims (consistent with federal law but adds state enforcement)
- Requires federal accreditation before assisting with claims
- Also enacted $20,000 military retirement pay tax exclusion (effective tax years 2025-2029)

**Legislative context:**
- Source: https://trackbill.com/s3/bills/CA/2025/SB/694/analyses/assembly-military-and-veterans-affairs.pdf
- 4 hours 39 minutes of legislative debate
- 99 speakers testified at public hearings in 2025
- California CVSOs filed 316,000 claims in 2024-2025, securing $687 million in benefits

**Impact reporting:**
- Source: https://calmatters.org/digital-democracy/2026/02/veterans-claims-consulting-law/
- Unaccredited companies were charging veterans thousands of dollars for services
  available free through CVSOs and VSOs
- One veteran reported $20,000 in back-pay charges to an unaccredited company

- A federal appeals court sided with Veterans Guardian when it challenged New Jersey's
  prohibition, creating legal uncertainty about state enforcement vs. federal preemption.
- Source: https://calmatters.org/digital-democracy/2026/02/veterans-claims-consulting-law/
- Implication: State laws may face constitutional challenges. VetAssist's "completely free"
  model avoids this entire legal battlefield.

## 3. The "Educational Tool" Safe Harbor

### VA OGC April 28, 2004 Opinion

**Cited by:** https://vetclaims.ai/38-cfr-va-attorney-agent-fees/

The VA Office of General Counsel clarified that services that do NOT constitute
"preparation, presentation, or prosecution of a claim" are permitted, including:

**PERMITTED (What VetAssist Does):**
- General educational services about the VA claims process
- Informational tools about eligibility requirements and rating criteria
- Self-help assistance empowering veterans to independently prepare their own claims
- Document quality review (grammar, clarity, formatting — like Grammarly)

**PROHIBITED (What VetAssist Does NOT Do):**
- Gathering specific evidence to support a particular claim
- Drafting claim forms or substantive statements to be filed with VA
- Otherwise directly assisting in the filing of a claim
- Any service where the provider directly participates in the preparation,
  submission, or prosecution of the claim

### VetAssist Compliance Architecture
Every feature in VetAssist must be validated against this boundary:

| Feature | Category | Legal Status |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| AI Chat (CFR Q&A) | Educational | Permitted |
| Document Review (Grammarly-style) | Self-help tool | Permitted |
| Document Templates | Self-help tool | Permitted |
| Benefits Discovery | Educational | Permitted |
| Community Stories | Peer support | Permitted |
| C&P Exam Tips | Educational | Permitted — with disclaimer |
| "Bigger Picture" Mode | Educational | Permitted — must NOT tell veteran what to file |
| Filing claims for veterans | Representation | PROHIBITED |
| Guaranteeing outcomes | Representation | PROHIBITED |
| Directly submitting forms to VA | Representation | PROHIBITED |

## 4. AI Regulation: Healthcare Chatbot Compliance (2026)

### California AB 489 (Effective January 1, 2026)
- Source: https://www.akerman.com/en/perspectives/hrx-new-year-new-ai-rules-healthcare-ai-laws-now-in-effect.html
- Prohibits AI systems from using terms or design elements implying healthcare licensure
- Healthcare licensing boards can pursue injunctions for violations
- VetAssist compliance: No medical imagery, no "Dr." prefixes, no white coat icons,
  no language suggesting the AI has medical training

### California SB 243 — Companion Chatbots (Effective January 1, 2026)
- Source: Same as above
- Requires clear notification that users are interacting with AI
- Mandates protocols to prevent responses encouraging self-harm
- Requires notification referring users to crisis services if suicidal ideation detected
- VetAssist compliance: AI disclosure banner (persistent), Veterans Crisis Line
  integration (immediate response to crisis language)

### Texas TRAIGA (Effective January 1, 2026)
- Source: Same Akerman article
- Broad AI governance requirements including disclosure obligations
- Requires healthcare providers to disclose AI use in relation to healthcare services
- VetAssist compliance: Texas users see AI transparency disclosures

### FTC Section 5 — Unfair or Deceptive Practices
- Source: https://drata.com/blog/artificial-intelligence-regulations-state-and-federal-ai-laws-2026
- Applies to AI systems making misleading claims about capabilities
- VetAssist compliance: Never claim AI provides legal advice, medical diagnosis,
  or guaranteed outcomes

### HIPAA Applicability Analysis
- Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10937180/
- Key finding: AI chatbots processing PHI on behalf of HIPAA covered entities
  become business associates requiring BAAs
- VetAssist analysis: VetAssist is NOT a HIPAA covered entity. Veterans enter
  their OWN information (not medical records from a provider). VetAssist falls
  under FTC Health Breach Notification Rule (16 CFR Part 318), not HIPAA.
- Source for FTC HBNR: https://www.nortonrosefulbright.com/en-us/knowledge/publications/55f5440a/navigating-ai-compliance-with-hipaa-essentials

### Business Associate Agreements
- Source: https://www.groovyweb.co/blog/healthcare-app-compliance-guide-2026
- Key finding: "Every third-party API you integrate — including LLM providers used
  in your healthcare chatbot — must have a signed BAA" IF handling PHI
- Anthropic Claude API: Offers BAA for healthcare enterprise customers
- VetAssist: Does NOT process PHI from covered entities. BAA not required for
  current architecture. If VetAssist ever connects to VA health records, BAA
  would become required.

## 5. Data Privacy: PII Protection Standards

### VA Privacy Requirements
- Source: https://department.va.gov/privacy/fact-sheet/what-va-is-doing-to-protect-your-privacy/
- Privacy Act of 1974: Covers federal government collection, maintenance, use,
  and disclosure of personal information
- HIPAA Privacy Rule: Standardizes protections for public and private healthcare facilities
- VA Privacy Service: Central organization overseeing all VA privacy efforts since 2002

### VA Mobile App Compliance Requirements
- Source: https://mobile.va.gov/va-mobile-app-compliance-requirements
- Apps processing veteran ePHI/PII must implement:
  - Password/PIN or appropriate access control
  - FIPS 140-2 validated encryption for stored ePHI/PII
  - FIPS 140-2 validated encryption for transmitted ePHI/PII
  - Removal mechanisms to sanitize stored ePHI/PII
- Note: These requirements apply to apps in the VA App Store. VetAssist is an
  independent educational app, not a VA App Store submission. However, implementing
  these standards voluntarily demonstrates commitment to veteran data protection.

### VA Privacy Impact Assessment (2026)
- Source: https://department.va.gov/privacy/wp-content/uploads/sites/5/2026/03/FY26VAEMobileApplicationPlatformCloudAssessingPIA.pdf
- VHA uses PII and PHI only as legally permitted
- Written authorizations required prior to releasing, disclosing, or sharing PII/PHI
- Individuals have right to restrict disclosure and use of their health information

### Microsoft Presidio for PII Detection
- Source: https://www.philschmid.de/pii-huggingface-sagemaker
- Integration pattern: Presidio + Hugging Face Transformers for enhanced PII detection
- Presidio supports 24+ PII entity types out of the box (credit cards, IBAN, email,
  US bank numbers, ITINs, SSNs)
- Can be extended with custom transformer models for domain-specific entities
  (VA file numbers, military service numbers)

### NVIDIA Nemotron-PII Dataset
- Source: https://huggingface.co/datasets/nvidia/Nemotron-PII
- 55+ PII/PHI entity categories
- Covers both structured (forms, invoices) and unstructured (emails, free text)
- Useful for fine-tuning PII detection models on veteran document language

## 6. Data Encryption: Federal Standards

### FIPS 140-2 / FIPS 140-3
- Source: https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-module-validation-program
- FIPS 140-2 is the US/Canadian government standard for cryptographic modules
- FIPS 140-2 expires September 21, 2026 — transition to FIPS 140-3 required
- Source: https://112cyber.com/blog/fips-encryption-requirements-in-cmmc-and-nist-sp-800-171/

### NIST SP 800-171
- Source: https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/sp/800-171/rev-2/final
- 110 security controls for protecting Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)
- Control 3.13.11: "Employ FIPS-validated cryptography when used to protect
  the confidentiality of CUI"
- While VetAssist may not handle CUI directly, implementing NIST 800-171
  controls demonstrates industry-best-practice security

### VetAssist Encryption Implementation
- Data at rest: AES-256 encryption for all stored files (temporary encrypted storage)
- Data in transit: TLS 1.3 minimum on all connections
- Key management: Encryption keys in environment variables, never in source code
- Implementation note: Node.js crypto module uses OpenSSL which has FIPS-validated
  builds available. For strict FIPS compliance, use OpenSSL FIPS provider.

## 7. Accessibility: WCAG, Section 508, and VA Directive 6221

### WCAG 2.1 Level AA
- Source: https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG21/
- 50 success criteria at Level AA (including all Level A criteria)
- Four principles: Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust
- Mobile-specific criteria added in 2.1 (not in 2.0):
  - 1.3.4 Orientation (don't restrict to portrait/landscape)
  - 1.3.5 Identify Input Purpose (autocomplete attributes)
  - 1.4.10 Reflow (content works at 320px width without horizontal scrolling)
  - 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast (3:1 ratio for UI components)
  - 1.4.12 Text Spacing (respect user text spacing preferences)
  - 1.4.13 Content on Hover or Focus (dismissable, hoverable, persistent)
  - 2.1.4 Character Key Shortcuts (can be remapped or disabled)
  - 2.5.1 Pointer Gestures (single-pointer alternatives for multipoint gestures)
  - 2.5.2 Pointer Cancellation (up-event activation)
  - 2.5.3 Label in Name (visible label matches accessible name)
  - 2.5.4 Motion Actuation (alternatives to device motion)
  - 4.1.3 Status Messages (programmatically determinable without focus)

### Section 508 (Revised 2017)
- Source: https://www.section508.gov/develop/applicability-conformance/
- Incorporates WCAG 2.0 Level AA by reference
- Applies to federal agencies AND vendors providing ICT to federal agencies
- Source: https://www.access-board.gov/ict/wcag2ict.html

### VA Directive 6221
- Source: https://www.devvly.com/entry/section-508-protecting-disabled-veterans
- VA-wide policy ensuring employees and public with disabilities have access to VA ICT
- VA's Section 508 testing department uses WCAG 2.1 success criteria

### VetAssist Implementation
- Target: WCAG 2.1 Level AA (exceeds Section 508's WCAG 2.0 AA requirement)
- Testing: axe-core automated auditing in CI/CD pipeline
- Manual testing: Screen reader verification (VoiceOver, TalkBack, NVDA)
- Color contrast: 4.5:1 minimum for normal text, 3:1 for large text
- Touch targets: Minimum 48x48px (WCAG), preferred 56x56px (for elderly users)
- Font scaling: 16px-36px adjustable (exceeds 200% zoom requirement)

## 8. Content Moderation: Legal Requirements

### California SB 243 Crisis Protocols
- Companion chatbots must have protocols to prevent responses encouraging self-harm
- Must provide crisis service referrals when user expresses suicidal ideation
- Source: Akerman article cited in Section 4

### FTC Enforcement on User-Generated Content
- Platforms hosting user content must not make deceptive claims about moderation
- If you claim content is "verified," it must actually be verified
- Source: FTC Act Section 5

### CAN-SPAM Act (Email Distribution)
- Source: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business
- Requirements: Clear identification, honest subject lines, physical address,
  opt-out mechanism (honored within 10 business days), no harvested addresses

### VetAssist Implementation
- Automated moderation: toxic-bert + PII scrubber + custom rules
- Crisis detection: keyword + context analysis → immediate Veterans Crisis Line display
- Community reporting: anonymous report button on all user content
- Admin escalation: lockdown mode for manual approval of all posts
- Graduated response: warning → 24hr suspension → 7-day → permanent ban
- Email: opt-in only, unsubscribe in every email, max 4/month, CAN-SPAM compliant

## 10. Methodology: Sub-Agent Research Framework

### Purpose
This section defines how Claude Code should continue autonomous research
to fill gaps and keep the knowledge base current.

### Sub-Agent Definitions

**Agent 1: Federal Regulation Monitor**
- Scope: 38 CFR, 38 USC, Federal Register VA documents
- Schedule: Daily automated check
- Sources: eCFR API, Federal Register API
- Output: Change detection log, relevance scores, admin review queue

**Agent 2: State Law Tracker**
- Scope: State-level veteran protection legislation across all 50 states
- Schedule: Monthly review
- Sources: LegiScan API (if available), state legislature websites, Stateline.org
- Output: State law database updates, new legislation alerts

**Agent 3: AI Compliance Monitor**
- Scope: New AI regulations affecting healthcare/veteran chatbots
- Schedule: Monthly review
- Sources: Akerman health AI tracker, Manatt Health AI Policy Tracker,
  state legislature searches
- Output: Compliance requirement updates, feature adjustment recommendations

**Agent 4: Security Standards Monitor**
- Scope: NIST, FIPS, encryption standard updates
- Schedule: Quarterly review
- Sources: NIST CSRC, FIPS CMVP announcements
- Output: Security implementation recommendations
- Critical: FIPS 140-2 expires September 21, 2026 — monitor transition to 140-3

**Agent 5: Accessibility Standards Monitor**
- Scope: WCAG updates, Section 508 changes, VA Directive updates
- Schedule: Quarterly review
- Sources: W3C WAI, Section508.gov, VA accessibility announcements
- Output: Accessibility requirement updates

### Research Quality Standards
- Every claim must have a verifiable URL
- Prefer primary sources (eCFR, USC, VA.gov) over secondary analysis
- Date-stamp all research findings
- Flag when sources conflict
- Mark confidence level: HIGH (primary source), MEDIUM (reputable secondary),
  LOW (community report — requires verification)
